About

I'm an AI researcher and engineer who takes systems from a research idea to something that runs — under real constraints, on real hardware, measured honestly. At KPST I shipped an infinite-stream industrial-safety vision-language model that watches never-ending camera feeds with bounded memory and emits schema-constrained hazard decisions to drive a live alarm system.

At KAIST's Knowledge & Democracy Lab I research LLM reasoning and difficulty perception, VLM bias and interpretability, and LLM safety — adversarial prefills, many-shot jailbreaking, model introspection. Earlier, at ICLab, I co-authored an IMWUT / Ubicomp 2024 reproducible mobile-sensing pipeline and led its personalization component. My strengths sit at the meeting point of PyTorch and Hugging Face fine-tuning, interpretability and evaluation-harness design, and reproducible experimentation.

Infinite-Memory Streaming VLM for Industrial Hazard Detection

KPST (Korea Platform Service Technology) — AI Researcher
Feb 2026 — Present

A real-time safety system that watches a continuous, never-ending camera stream and emits one compact, schema-constrained JSON hazard decision per step to drive a downstream alarm — no separate object detector, the VLM makes the call end to end.

  • Adapted Qwen2.5-VL-7B with StreamingVLM-style streaming attention (attention-sink + sliding-window KV cache, contiguous 3D RoPE) for constant memory and per-step latency regardless of stream duration — a camera can run for hours or days without KV growth or drift.
  • Designed a two-stage LoRA SFT pipeline (broad SFT, then event-centered annealing on hazard transitions) on a single V100 32 GB, freezing the vision tower.
  • Guaranteed 100% output validity via FSM / schema-constrained decoding, and measured generalization on held-out cameras never seen in training.

LLM Reasoning, Safety & VLM Interpretability

Knowledge & Democracy Lab, KAIST — Undergraduate Researcher
Aug 2025 — Feb 2026

Three research tracks across LLM reasoning, model safety, and vision-language interpretability — building reproducible pipelines end to end.

LLM Difficulty Perceptionsubmitted · ACL 2026

Built a sandboxed code-execution framework with strict I/O judging and a reasoning-eval suite over 4 benchmarks (658 problems) × 6 LLMs; implemented the LoRA/QLoRA fine-tuning pipeline (TRL + PEFT) and curated AIME / E2H-Codeforces SFT data.

LLM Safety & IntrospectionCOLM 2026 · arXiv

Built a reproducible SGLang + OpenRouter pipeline evaluating prefill attacks (control / static / adversarial) and model introspection over HarmBench & SocialHarmBench; implemented ADVPrefix end to end and reproduced Anthropic's Many-Shot Jailbreaking (Llama Guard scoring) across Gemma / Qwen / Llama. Key finding — across 10 open-weight LLMs (3B–70B), no model reliably recognizes its own adversarially-prefilled outputs. Read the preprint ↗

VLM Ideology, Interpretability & Bias Localization

Extended political-ideology probing from LLMs to Vision-Language Models: trained per-layer/per-head ridge-regression linear probes on DW-NOMINATE scores, performed inference-time steering by injecting learned ideology vectors, built a ~550-politician multimodal dataset (Voteview + portraits), and ran NMF topic modeling over chain-of-thought to surface the visual cues models tie to ideology. Extended the same probing to localize bias — measuring sensitive-attribute leakage and spurious correlations in InternVL, Gemma3-VL, Qwen-VL via layer-wise probes and attribution (Grad-CAM, Integrated Gradients, TCAV), and applying counterfactual edits to quantify subgroup gaps and localize bias to image regions and cross-modal tokens.

Mobile-Sensing for Mental Health

ICLab, KAIST — Undergraduate Researcher
May 2023 — Jun 2025

Four research tracks on in-the-wild stress prediction from multimodal phone/wearable sensor data — reproducible pipelines, leakage guards, and cross-user generalization.

Reproducible Stress-Prediction PipelineACM IMWUT 2024

Co-authored a reproducible in-the-wild stress-prediction pipeline (feature extraction, leakage guards, temporal-order controls). Led the personalization component — partial / multi-task personalization via an adapted TSGB multi-task learner — improving AUROC by up to ~17% on DeepStress over the user-independent baseline.

CrossShift — Inter-Personal DifferencesACM IMWUT 2026

Built CrossShift, quantifying user-level data shift across 8 datasets / 693 users: PERMANOVA/PERMDISP and χ² testing with Wasserstein (covariate), L2 (label), OTDD (conditional), and JS-divergence (concept) quantification, plus a LOSO harness and domain-generalization baselines — finding concept shift P(Y|X) the primary driver of cross-user degradation (β = 0.265), and using JS-based OOD checks for onboarding-time selective personalization.

CausalCFF — Causal Analysis of StressCHI EA 2025

Designed the CausalCFF pipeline — POI clustering, association-rule mining, and Convergent Cross Mapping causal analysis — surfacing high-lift behavior patterns and testing their causal impact on stress across six weeks of GPS / activity / app-usage data; structured and documented the codebase for public release.

Multimodal Sensor Integration

Integrated multimodal sensor data (Audio, Aqara, Withings, Smartphone) with KNN imputation and feature selection (ANOVA, LASSO-CV, correlation filtering); identified 60-minute windows and "Yesterday Epoch" features as the strongest configuration via LOSO and K-Fold CV with hyperparameter optimization.
